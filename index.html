Market Context

When property owners use seller financing, they create income-producing financial assets. Real estate notes consist of a promissory note secured by a mortgage or deed of trust. Land contracts allow the seller to retain title until payment is complete.

In 2024, there were approximately 90,000 seller-financed transactions nationwide totaling more than $30 billion in volume. However, most private note holders lack the systems, documentation, and compliance infrastructure to manage these investments professionally.

The Challenge

Without professional infrastructure, private note holders face significant operational and financial risks.

Documentation Gaps

Missing documentation creates title defects that reduce asset value and complicate future sales.

Manual Tracking Errors

Manual payment tracking leads to errors in tax reporting and compliance violations.

Limited Liquidity

Disorganized portfolios become difficult to refinance, sell, or use as collateral.

Growth Ceiling

Spreadsheet systems break down entirely as portfolios grow, creating an artificial ceiling on growth.

Our Solution

Core Assets Management provides institutional-grade organization and management tailored for private note investors. We bring automation, data structure, and professional oversight to transform notes tracked in spreadsheets and filing cabinets into scalable, liquid investment assets.
